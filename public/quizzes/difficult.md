# Difficult Level - Cell Biology (6 Items)

## Question 1 (Multiple Choice) - Chloroplast

**Passage:**
A plant kept in darkness for several weeks weakens. Chloroplasts cannot capture sunlight to make glucose, reducing energy for growth. Lack of photosynthesis slows metabolic processes and weakens the plant.

**Question:** Why does the plant weaken in the dark?

- **A. Chloroplasts cannot perform photosynthesis** (correct)
- B. Mitochondria stop producing energy
- C. The nucleus disappears
- D. Vacuoles burst

**Justification:** Chloroplasts need sunlight to produce food; without it, energy production and growth are limited.

**Hint:** What process requires sunlight, and what organelle performs it?

## Question 2 (Multiple Choice) - Cell Membrane

**Passage:**
A cell placed in saltwater shrinks as water moves out by osmosis. The cytoplasm decreases in volume and the cell appears smaller. This shows the effect of solute concentration on cell size.

**Question:** What happened to the water inside the cell?

- **A. Water moved out of the cell** (correct)
- B. Water moved into the cell
- C. The membrane thickened
- D. The vacuole expanded

**Justification:** Water moves from high to low concentration, leaving the cell in a salty solution.

**Hint:** The cell SHRINKS in saltwater. Where did the water go?

## Question 3 (Multiple Choice) - Mitochondrion

**Passage:**
Mitochondria release energy from nutrients in the form of ATP. Damaged mitochondria slow or stop energy production. Cells cannot carry out energy-dependent functions efficiently.

**Question:** Which life process is most affected if mitochondria are damaged?

- **A. Energy release from food** (correct)
- B. Control of cell activities
- C. Water storage
- D. Protection and support

**Justification:** ATP production is impaired, limiting all energy-requiring processes.

**Hint:** What is the main job of mitochondria? That process would be most affected.

## Question 4 (Multiple Choice) - Nucleus

**Passage:**
A cell has chloroplasts but no nucleus. It can perform photosynthesis but cannot divide or regulate internal functions properly. The cell is incomplete but shows some plant features.

**Question:** How should this cell be classified?

- **A. Immature or incomplete plant cell** (correct)
- B. Typical plant cell
- C. Animal cell
- D. Bacterial cell

**Justification:** Without a nucleus, the cell cannot fully control activities or divide, making it incomplete.

**Hint:** Can a cell be "complete" if it's missing the organelle that controls all its activities?

## Question 5 (True/False) - Cell

**Passage:**
Cells in multicellular organisms specialize to perform different tasks. Nerve cells transmit signals, muscle cells contract, and leaf cells capture sunlight. Specialization improves survival and efficiency.

**Question:** Specialization of cells is unnecessary because all cells perform the same functions.

- True
- **False** (correct)

**Justification:** Specialization allows cells to efficiently perform distinct functions.

**Hint:** The passage gives examples of cells doing DIFFERENT tasks. Does that mean specialization is unnecessary?

## Question 6 (Multiple Choice) - Cell Wall

**Passage:**
A plant cell in freshwater swells as water enters by osmosis. The rigid cell wall prevents bursting and maintains the shape of the cell. This structure supports the plant under varying water conditions.

**Question:** Which structure prevents the plant cell from bursting?

- **A. Cell wall** (correct)
- B. Cytoplasm
- C. Vacuole
- D. Ribosome

**Justification:** The cell wall resists pressure from incoming water, keeping the cell intact.

**Hint:** What rigid structure surrounds the plant cell and prevents it from expanding too much?
