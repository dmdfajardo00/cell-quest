**CELL QUEST QUESTIONS & JUSTIFICATION** 

**EASY LEVEL** (7 Items)

1\. (Multiple Choice)

All living organisms are made of cells, which are the smallest units that carry out life processes such as growth, metabolism, and reproduction. Cells form the foundation of life, whether in single-celled or multicellular organisms. This is why the cell is considered the basic unit of life.

Question: What is the basic unit of life?  
A. Tissue  
B. Cell  
C. System  
D. Organ

Answer: B. Cell  
Justification: Cells perform all essential life processes and form the foundation of every organism.

2\. (Multiple Choice)

Each cell has a nucleus, which contains DNA that stores the instructions needed for growth, division, and normal functioning. The nucleus controls the activities of the cell and ensures that it reproduces correctly. This makes it the control center of the cell.

Question: Which organelle contains the genetic material?  
A. Cytoplasm  
B. Nucleus  
C. Ribosome  
D. Vacuole

Answer: B. Nucleus  
Justification: DNA in the nucleus directs cell functions and carries hereditary information.

3\. (True/False)

The cell membrane surrounds the cell and regulates the entry and exit of substances. It maintains the internal balance of the cell, allowing it to function properly. Without a functioning membrane, the cell cannot control its internal environment.

Question: The cell membrane regulates the movement of materials into and out of the cell.  
Answer: True  
Justification: The membrane ensures proper balance and prevents harmful substances from entering the cell.

4\. (Multiple Choice)

Cells need energy to survive and perform activities. Mitochondria release energy from nutrients, producing ATP that powers the cell. This energy allows the cell to grow, repair, and carry out vital functions.

Question: Which organelle produces energy for the cell?  
A. Vacuole  
B. Cell wall  
C. Mitochondrion  
D. Chloroplast

Answer: C. Mitochondrion  
Justification: Mitochondria convert nutrients into usable energy for cell activities.

5\. (True/False)

Plant cells have a cell wall, which gives the cell a defined shape and supports the plant. Animal cells lack a cell wall and rely on other structures for support. The cell wall is therefore a distinctive feature of plant cells.

Question: Animal cells have a cell wall.

Answer: False  
Justification: Only plant cells have cell walls for structural support; animal cells do not.

6\. (Multiple Choice)

Cells perform all necessary life functions, including metabolism, growth, and reproduction. Each cell is capable of carrying out these processes independently. This makes the cell the fundamental unit of life.

Question: Why is the cell considered the basic unit of life?  
A. Cells perform essential life processes  
B. Life exists without cells  
C. Only plants have cells  
D. Cells are the largest structures in organisms

Answer: A  
Justification: Cells perform all vital functions needed for survival.

7\. (Multiple Choice)

Plant cells are often rectangular because of a rigid cell wall, which provides structural support. This wall helps the plant maintain its shape. It also allows the cells to form tissues that support the plant body.

Question: Why do plant cells usually have a rectangular shape?  
A. They have a cell wall  
B. They lack cytoplasm  
C. They do not contain organelles  
D. They have no nucleus

Answer: A  
Justification: The rigid cell wall provides shape and support to plant cells.

**MODERATE LEVEL** (7 Items)

1\. (Multiple Choice)

Cells contain specialized structures called organelles. Mitochondria release energy from nutrients, while chloroplasts capture sunlight to make glucose. Each organelle has a unique function to support the survival of the cell.

Question: How does the function of mitochondria differ from chloroplasts?  
A. Mitochondria release energy from food while chloroplasts make food  
B. Both produce energy the same way  
C. Mitochondria store water while chloroplasts digest food  
D. Chloroplasts control cell activities

Answer: A  
Justification: Mitochondria produce energy from food, while chloroplasts synthesize glucose from sunlight.

2\. (Multiple Choice)

In multicellular organisms, cells specialize in different roles. Muscle cells contract, nerve cells transmit signals, and leaf cells capture sunlight. This specialization ensures efficient functioning of the organism.

Question: Why do multicellular organisms have specialized cells?  
A. To perform specific functions efficiently  
B. To make cells larger  
C. To reduce energy use  
D. To avoid using organelles

Answer: A  
Justification: Specialization allows cells to efficiently carry out specific roles.

3\. (True/False)

The cell membrane controls the movement of substances in and out of the cell. Damage to it prevents the cell from maintaining internal balance. Without this regulation, the cell’s survival is compromised.

Question: If the cell membrane is damaged, the cell can still regulate what enters and leaves.

Answer: False  
Justification: A damaged membrane cannot control material movement, disrupting cellular function.

4\.  Multiple Choice)

A cell observed under a microscope has a cell wall and chloroplasts. It can make its own food through photosynthesis and maintains a rigid structure. These features are absent in animal cells.

Question: Based on its structures and functions, which type of cell is this?  
A. Plant cell  
B. Animal cell  
C. Bacterial cell  
D. Muscle cell

Answer: A  
Justification: Chloroplasts for photosynthesis and a rigid cell wall are unique characteristics of plant cells.

5\. (True/False)

Cells in a tissue communicate using chemical signals to coordinate their functions. Without this coordination, tissues cannot perform properly. Proper communication ensures organs can function efficiently.

Question: Communication between cells is unnecessary for tissue function.

Answer: False  
Justification: Coordination is required for tissues to perform their roles effectively.

6\. (Multiple Choice)

During exercise, muscle cells require energy to sustain contraction. Mitochondria produce ATP, the energy molecule needed for movement. Cells with more active mitochondria can maintain longer activity.

Question: Which organelle becomes most active during intense exercise?  
A. Mitochondrion  
B. Ribosome  
C. Vacuole  
D. Cell wall

Answer: A  
Justification: Mitochondria supply energy required for sustained muscle activity.

7\. (Multiple Choice)

Plant cells contain a large vacuole filled with water and nutrients. It helps maintain internal pressure and supports the plant body. This structure also stores materials needed for survival.

Question: Why are plant vacuoles larger than those in animal cells?  
A. To store water and nutrients  
B. To help the cell move  
C. To control cell division  
D. To produce proteins

Answer: A  
Justification: Large vacuoles maintain turgor pressure and store essential substances.

**DIFFICULT LEVEL** (6 Items)

1\.  Multiple Choice)

A plant kept in darkness for several weeks weakens. Chloroplasts cannot capture sunlight to make glucose, reducing energy for growth. Lack of photosynthesis slows metabolic processes and weakens the plant.

Question: Why does the plant weaken in the dark?  
A. Chloroplasts cannot perform photosynthesis  
B. Mitochondria stop producing energy  
C. The nucleus disappears  
D. Vacuoles burst

Answer: A  
Justification: Chloroplasts need sunlight to produce food; without it, energy production and growth are limited.

2\.  Multiple Choice)

A cell placed in saltwater shrinks as water moves out by osmosis. The cytoplasm decreases in volume and the cell appears smaller. This shows the effect of solute concentration on cell size.

Question: What happened to the water inside the cell?  
A. Water moved out of the cell  
B. Water moved into the cell  
C. The membrane thickened  
D. The vacuole expanded

Answer: A  
Justification: Water moves from high to low concentration, leaving the cell in a salty solution.

3\.  Multiple Choice)

Mitochondria release energy from nutrients in the form of ATP. Damaged mitochondria slow or stop energy production. Cells cannot carry out energy-dependent functions efficiently.

Question: Which life process is most affected if mitochondria are damaged?  
A. Energy release from food  
B. Control of cell activities  
C. Water storage  
D. Protection and support

Answer: A  
Justification: ATP production is impaired, limiting all energy-requiring processes.

4\.  Multiple Choice)

A cell has chloroplasts but no nucleus. It can perform photosynthesis but cannot divide or regulate internal functions properly. The cell is incomplete but shows some plant features.

Question: How should this cell be classified?  
A. Immature or incomplete plant cell  
B. Typical plant cell  
C. Animal cell  
D. Bacterial cell

Answer: A  
Justification: Without a nucleus, the cell cannot fully control activities or divide, making it incomplete.

5\. (True/False)

Cells in multicellular organisms specialize to perform different tasks. Nerve cells transmit signals, muscle cells contract, and leaf cells capture sunlight. Specialization improves survival and efficiency.

Question: Specialization of cells is unnecessary because all cells perform the same functions.

Answer: False  
Justification: Specialization allows cells to efficiently perform distinct functions.

6\.  Multiple Choice)

A plant cell in freshwater swells as water enters by osmosis. The rigid cell wall prevents bursting and maintains the shape of the cell. This structure supports the plant under varying water conditions.

Question: Which structure prevents the plant cell from bursting?  
A. Cell wall  
B. Cytoplasm  
C. Vacuole  
D. Ribosome

Answer: A  
Justification: The cell wall resists pressure from incoming water, keeping the cell intact.
